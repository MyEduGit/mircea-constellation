# MONETIZATION — Whom, What, How

*Strategy branch: `claude/monetization-strategy-KICDO` · drafted 2026-04-17*

Purpose: fund Mircea (rent, food, hardware, compute) and the Mission
(*The Urantia Book reaching every human being*) from assets that already
exist in the constellation. No new building. Sell what is built.

Read this top-down. Each row answers the three questions:

1. **WHAT** — the asset someone can take over and run.
2. **WHOM** — the party most likely to pay / host / license.
3. **HOW** — the exact opening line of the ask.

---

## Principle: separate stewardship from ownership

You are not selling the Mission. You are selling **stewardship rights**
(operate it, host it, distribute it, teach from it) while retaining
**copyright + veto**. Most of the takers below want to *operate*, not
*own*. Price accordingly.

- **License, don't assign.** 5-year renewable, revocable for cause.
- **Revenue share, not lump sum**, where possible — aligns incentives.
- **Father Function veto** on anything doctrinal (Urantia content, UrantiOS).
- **Lucifer Test applies to the buyer too** — if they fail it, decline
  the money.

## What NOT to sell

- **The Book itself.** Public domain. Selling it violates the Mission.
- **Doctrinal control over UrantiPedia or UrantiOS.** License operations, not interpretation.
- **Exclusive rights to anything forever.** All licenses 3–5 year, renewable.
- **Student data from AMEP.** Aggregates only. Never individual records.
- **Your name or endorsement** to buyers who fail the Lucifer Test.

---

## Truth · Beauty · Goodness check

Every deal above is judged against:

- **Truth** — does the buyer represent what they are, and what they'll do with it?
- **Beauty** — does the arrangement preserve the shape of the Mission, or disfigure it?
- **Goodness** — does the money actually reach the people it should (you, the Mission, the students)?

If any of the three fails, walk. The covenant is upstream of the money.
