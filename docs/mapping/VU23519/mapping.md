## Application / Description

This unit describes the performance outcomes, skills and knowledge required by EAL learners to participate in simple conversations and transactions in everyday contexts.

This unit is part of the 22639VIC Certificate II in EAL (Access), which focuses on the development of English language speaking, listening, reading and writing skills directly related to immediate personal and social needs.

## Assessment Task Bank (Validated for Term 1, 2026)

### Task A: A New Student
- **Scenario:** Casual conversation helping a new student settle into the class
- **Evidence:** Opening expressions ("Hi, how are you?"), describing the class and local facilities
- **Maps to:** PC 1.1-1.6 (Element 1 - Conversations)

### Task B: Sales Assistant (Refund)
- **Scenario:** Simple transaction requesting a refund for a faulty item
- **Evidence:** Requesting a refund, explaining the problem with the item
- **Maps to:** PC 2.1-2.6 (Element 2 - Transactions)

### Task C: Bus Stop
- **Scenario:** Casual chat with a friend about the weather, bus service, and daily plans
- **Evidence:** Informal language, turn-taking, topic management

## Knowledge Evidence (KE) - Across the Unit

The assessor must record verbatim evidence of students using:
- **Phrasal verbs:** e.g., "pick up," "looking after," "put on"
- **Conjunctions:** e.g., "when," "but," "if"
- **Discourse markers:** e.g., "by the way," "anyway"
- **Polite transactional expressions:** e.g., "Excuse me," "I'd like to..."
- **Simple sentence structures:** Subject-verb-object patterns

## Required Skills

The candidate must demonstrate the ability to:
- Initiate and respond in simple face-to-face conversations
- Use appropriate opening and closing conventions
- Participate in simple transactions for everyday goods and services

## Required Knowledge

The candidate must demonstrate knowledge of:
- Common phrasal verbs in everyday contexts
- Simple conjunctions and discourse markers
- Polite expressions for transactions
- Simple sentence structures

## Assessment Administration

- Assessor must keep all documents in the task set secure
- Assessor must not allow learners to keep corrected response sheets after feedback
- Record verbatim quotes on KE checklists
- Provide verbal or written feedback
- Mark final result as "Satisfactory" or "More evidence required"
